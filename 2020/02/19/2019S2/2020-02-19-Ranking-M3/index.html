The Elo System

Posted on 2020-02-19 In Ranking Research

The Elo Method

Main Idea: Every competitor in a pool has a rating that determines their chance of winning against any other player as a function of the difference.

A player’s performance is along a normal distribution, with a mean, μ\muμ, that changes over time. The Elo ratings in a system should have a constant sum.

Adjustment

Originally Elo proposed that a player’s rating, rrr, should change according to a linear rule: rnew=rold+K(S−μ)r_{\text{new}}=r_{\text{old}}+K(S-\mu)rnew​=rold​+K(S−μ), where SSS is the player’s performance rating and KKK is a constant (Elo originally chose 10). He assumed that performance would be normally distributed, but with the advent of more statistical analysis that turned out to not be the case. USCF and FIDE both started using a more complicated logistic function.

We like matrices, so let’s make everything a matrix. SijS_{ij}Sij​ is 1 if iii beats jjj, 0 if jjj beats iii and 12\frac{1}{2}21​ otherwise and then μij\mu_{ij}μij​ becomes the amount of points iii is expected to get playing jjj. That expected value is determined with a logistic function based on the difference in rating, dij=ri−rjd_{ij}=r_i-r_jdij​=ri​−rj​. The function is

L(d)=110+10−d/400L(d)=\dfrac{1}{10+10^{-d/400}} L(d)=10+10−d/4001​

(notably chess uses base 10 instead of eee)

where 400 is a scaling interval.

The elegance of the system is that if a player has a lower chance of winning then they will make greater gains.

K-factor

The value of KKK has been long disputed, current K=40K=40K=40 for provisional players (less than 30 games), K=20K=20K=20 until a player reaches 2400 for the first time, then K=10K=10K=10 afterwards. Also, K=40K=40K=40 for all players younger than 18 until they hit 2300. Conversely, in blitz and rapid K=20K=20K=20 regardless of other factors. A high KKK value makes ratings overly volatile, but a low KKK value results in stagnancy.

In some other fields, such as soccer, KKK varies relative to the importance of the match.

Logistic Parameter

In the chess rating the division by 400, where ϵ=400\epsilon=400ϵ=400 is the logistic parameter. It affects the spread of ratings. For every ϵ\epsilonϵ that a player’s rating is higher they are expected to be ten times better than their opponent.

Performance Rating

While the system can be use for long term ratings, it can also be use to determine a short-term “performance rating.” The general method is to take a sum, for each game that you win add your opponent’s rating plus 400, then for each game lost add your opponent’s rating minus 400, then divide by the total number of games.

This is a rather simple way of calculating performance ratings, FIDE instead does

(Average Opponent)+(Rating Difference)(\text{Average Opponent}) + (\text{Rating Difference}) (Average Opponent)+(Rating Difference)

where rating difference, dpd_pdp​, is from a lookup table that takes in ppp, the points acquired out of total possible points.

Weird Chess Facts

USCF maintains a rating floor of 100, but that can change depending on certain events.

USCF has a title called “Original Life Master” that sets the recipients rating floor to 2200. For players rated below 2000 winning a cash price of more than 2000 USD results in a rating floor of the qualifying rating for the tournament.
