Polar Dialectrics

Composed of polar molecules, and will thus align with electric field.

Nonpolar Dialectrics

Dialectrics

The induced E-field points in the opposite direction. The cancellation is not perfect as it would be in a conductor, so it does not entirely cancel the original field.

The strength of the final field is E⃗=Efreeκ\vec{E}=\dfrac{E_\text{free}}{\kappa}E=κEfree​​ where κ≥1\kappa\geq1κ≥1 is the constant of the dialectric material. Vacuum has κ=1\kappa=1κ=1 and conductors (theoretically) have κ=∞\kappa=\inftyκ=∞.

Capacitance

With a dialectric, a capacitor stores the same charge at a lower voltage. Thus, dialectrics increase capacitance.
