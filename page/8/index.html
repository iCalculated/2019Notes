Review

  • Monopolies have MR < P
    • Will not be allocatively or productively efficient
    • Nonzero DWL

How Evil is Monopoly?

  • Good:
    • Utilizes economy of scale
    • Could be natural monopoly
      • Def’n: Does not make sense to have multiple firms because of startup price
  • Bad:
    • X-inefficiency: Not focused on minizing price of production because no competition
    • Rent-Seeking: Effort goes towards other causes, such as lobbying

Natural Monopoly

  • High one-time startup cost
    • Thus MC<<ATCMC << ATCMC<<ATC
  • Machine learning 😦

Regulation

  • Sherman Act (1890): Attempting to monopolize is uncool.
  • Clayton Act (1914): Merging to monopolize is uncool.
  • When splitting a monopoly, force MC=DMC=DMC=D.
    • Oh no, monopoly dies. (ATC<MBATC < MBATC<MB)
  • More reasonably, produce at ATC=DATC=DATC=D potentially with a percentage-based margin.
    • Oh no, monopoly still does not need to cut costs.

Price Discrimination

  • Discrimination → maximum profit.
  • Conditions:
    • Must be able to control output quantity or price
    • Must be willing able to segregate
    • No resale
  • Discriminate by elasticity
    • Assuming constant MC and zero FC
    • Segment after consumers are satisfied
  • Implemmentation
    • Offer rebate coupons, unlikely to be used by upper-class
      • Exploits both sensitivity and prevents resale

Review

  • Allocative efficiency because benefit to society (price) is equal to cost of production (MC)
  • Zero profit, since P=ATCP=ATCP=ATC
  • Productive efficiency because production occurs at the minimum possible cost

Monopoly

  • Entry is blocked
    • Thus profit exists
  • Marginal revenue is lower than price
    • Selling additional units requires a lower price
    • Because monopoly owns industry vs price-taking
  • Single seller with no close substitute
    • Usually because entry was blocked, patents and such
  • Can be a “fringe” when not the entire market is controlled but enough to practically influence it
  • Natural Monopoly: When economy of scale creates a monopoly through high initial cost.

Profit Maximization

  • Monopoly chooses unit elastic point on demand curve.
    • (this took about 20 minutes to explain, but was thankfully self-evident)
  • When MC is actually nonzero find intersection of MB and MC

Dead-Weight Loss

  • Socially we all suffer because products are priced higher at lower quantity
    • Not productively or allocatively efficientl
